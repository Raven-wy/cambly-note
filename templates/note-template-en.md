## Example structure

```markdown
## 📒 20260115_Michael_Brown

**Date**: Jan 15, 2026 ｜ **Tutor**: Michael Brown ｜ **Duration**: 45 min
**Stats**: Speaking ratio 62% ｜ Speed 98 wpm ｜ Unique vocab 187

### Topics

1. Giving directions and describing commute routes in English
2. Phrasal Verbs deep dive: verbs with "get"
3. Synonyms: speak / talk / say / tell

### Grammar Corrections

| What you said | Correct form | Note |
|---------------|-------------|------|
| I am go to... | I am going to... | "be going to" requires the -ing form |

### Phrasal Verbs Deep Dive

| Phrasal Verb | Meaning | Tutor's note | Example |
|-------------|---------|-------------|---------|
| get up | to rise from bed / stand up | Emphasizes the physical movement from lying/sitting | I get up at 7 every day. |

### 🗣️ Tutor's Natural Expressions

- **"You're getting there."** — Encouraging phrase; more natural than "Good job"; implies progress toward a goal

### Useful Vocab & Phrases

| Vocab / Phrase | Meaning | Context |
|---------------|---------|---------|
| take a detour | to take a longer alternative route | Tutor introduced it while discussing commute routes |

### Tutor Feedback

- ✅ Strengths: Fluent direction-giving with accurate vocabulary
- ⚠️ Areas to improve: Still mixing up "be going to" and "will"
- 💡 Practice tips: Describe a route to yourself in English every day
```
